Churn increased significantly over time, rising from single-digit monthly levels in early 2023 to a peak of 96 in Dec 2024, indicating a rapidly growing retention problem.
Revenue loss due to churn totaled $8.65K, with 25% of losses occurring in Dec 2024 alone, showing that recent churn is more financially impactful.
Feature-related issues are the primary churn driver (114 cases) and also contribute the highest revenue loss ($1.9K), highlighting clear product gaps.
Pricing and perceived cost (“expensive”) are major concerns, supported by high feedback mentions (161), indicating a strong value vs cost mismatch.
Competitive pressure is significant, with 136 mentions, suggesting users are actively comparing alternatives and switching platforms.
Only 8.83% of users downgraded before churn, indicating churn is often not gradual, but rather a direct decision driven by dissatisfaction.
However, 20.5% of users upgraded before churning, suggesting unmet expectations after upgrade and gaps in value realization or onboarding.
Reactivation rate is relatively low (~10%), indicating that most churn is permanent, though feature and budget-related churns show some recovery potential.
Churn in recent months shows both higher volume and higher revenue impact, making it a critical business risk requiring immediate intervention.
Overall, churn is primarily driven by a combination of product limitations, pricing concerns, and competitive alternatives, rather than isolated issues.
